Shaktipat Initiation

In the Master-disciple (Guru-Shishya) tradition, initiation is a rule. Among all types of initiations, ‘Shaktipat’ initiation is the best.

  • In ‘Shaktipat’ initiation, Guru by his own wish awakens the kundalini of the seeker by any one of the four ways:

    • By Touch
    • By a Divine Word (Mantra)
    • By Sight
    • By Resolve

  • The initiation in which the seeker has a direct experience of GOD immediately after the initiation is known as ‘Shambhavi’ initiation. This is a great initiation. Very few seekers have the ability to bear the effect of power (Divine energy) of this initiation. Such seekers have been called ‘Bhavpratyaya Yogi’ in Patanjali Yoga Philosophy.

  • Initiation by Touch: Guru flows his power into the seeker by touching at the Third Eye spot, heart centre and at the base of the spinal column at ‘Mooladhaar’ in this initiation.

  • Initiation by Word (Mantra): The power of Guru flows into the seeker through a ‘Mantra’. The initiation ‘Mantra’ that is given by the Guru has already been chanted by the Guru for a long period of time. The power of the Mantra has been absorbed by the Guru. There is no difference between that Mantra and the Guru. The entire body of the Guru becomes like the Mantra. When the Guru gives initiation by such an empowered Mantra, it gives liberation.

  • Initiation by Sight: This means an initiation given by merely looking at the seeker. The sight of a Guru who gives this kind of initiation is ‘antar-lakshi’. This kind of initiation can be given by only that Guru who has himself been initiated by an empowered Spiritual Master and he himself should also be ‘antar-lakshi’ otherwise it is impossible to give this kind of initiation.

Although the eyes of such great saints remain open but in reality, their attention is always on the Supreme Power. One can make out by looking at the pictures of such saints. There have been many such great saints like Saint Gurudev Shri Gurunanak Dev ji Maharaj, Saint Shri Kabir Das ji, Shri Ram Krishna Paramhans etc. who have appeared on this sacred land (referring to India).
